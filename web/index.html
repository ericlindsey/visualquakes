SimpleQuakes Okada 1985 · about

This page computes the surface deformation produced by a single rectangular fault (a dislocation in an elastic half-space, Okada 1985) and shows it the way a satellite radar would — as InSAR fringes.

The whole solution runs per pixel on your GPU in a WebGL2 fragment shader, so moving a slider just updates a few numbers and the image redraws in real time. Each color cycle in the default view is one fringe = λ/2 of motion toward/away from the satellite.

The fault's outline is drawn on the surface; the white edge marks its top (shallowest) edge. The in-browser engine is validated against a double-precision Python reference. Source & details: github.com/ericlindsey/SimpleQuakes.
